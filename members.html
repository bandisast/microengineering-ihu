Team Members

Michail E. Kiziroglou - Assistant Professor
Michail E. Kiziroglou received the diploma degree in Electrical and Computer Engineering from the Aristotle University of Thessaloniki, Thessaloniki, Greece, in 2000; the master's degree in Microelectronics and Nanoelectronics from the Democritus University of Thrace, Komotini, Greece, in 2003; and the Ph.D. degree in Microelectronics and Spintronics from the University of Southampton, Southampton, U.K., in 2007. He is currently a Research Fellow with the Optical and Semiconductor Devices Group, Imperial College London, London, U.K., and an Assistant Professor with the Department of Industrial Engineering and Management, International Hellenic University, Greece. His research interests include energy harvesting devices, microengineering, and energy autonomous wireless sensors.


Asterios Bantis - Microengineering Website Maintainer
Asterios Bantis is a graduand of the department of Automation Engineering and specialises in System Administration and Embedded Systems Engineering. In the past, they've also contributed to the Aristotle Space & Aeronautics Team, the IHU's AutoROS Team, the Balkan Physics Olympiad, and the International Workshop on Microsystems. They're also the maintainer of this website. In their free time, they enjoy working on (not necessarily IT-related) personal projects, taking very long walks, or doing one of their hobbies. Topics that interest them academically — but are not directly related to their field — include astronomy, particle physics, linguistics, mathematics, and philosophy.
